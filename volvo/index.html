HMI

The interface, called IntelliSage Auto Pilot, allows drivers to activate and deactivate the autonomous mode through specially-designed paddles on the steering wheel.

Strategic control

When in autonomous mode, the driver has an overview of everything the car is doing. Previews of the actions the car is about to perform, such as overtaking, mean that changes in the movement on the car are not surprising.

Time circle

The amount of autonomous drive time available on a particular route is displayed in the Time Circle. As the time begins to run out the driver will be alerted that they must soon take back control. Drivers may alter their route to find more or less autonomous drive time.

The name C26 refers to the fact that the average daily commute time in North America is 26 minutes.

In 2017, the DriveMe project will make 100 autonomous cars available to consumers in Gothenburg, Sweden for use in everyday driving conditions.

The IntelliSafe Auto Pilot interface, found in the C26, will be available for the first time on 100 XC90 model cars that Volvo will make available for its Drive Me project in Gothenburg, Sweden in 2017. The interface was developed to oversee how drivers will transfer control to a car's autonomous driving mode in future cars.

The Drive Me project will make 100 self-driving Volvos available to consumers around Gothenburg for use in everyday driving conditions. The cars will be driven autonomously on about 50 kilometers (31 miles) of selected roads.
